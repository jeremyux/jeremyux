Law Office of dante cuddy

Designer and Web Developer

Dante Cuddy is a probate lawyer based in the bay area that wanted a revamp of not only his brand but work that into the website fopr a consistent experience. I have already worked on his logo and styleguide so takiong his compaany facade a little further I wanted to create a website to hlp him capture leads as well as point users to content that Dante has created to educate his future clients.

Before & After
I wasn't the first one to work on this project. Dante hired his brother who created the 1st iteration on the left. The following use case breaks down the methodology I used to create a website that would not only convert visitors but also educate them as well. To start building the foundation to this website's visual language I needed to incorporate the branding work I did for Dante which you can see here.

Branding
Now that I have the colors, typography and styleguide sorted out I had to figure out the methodology when organizing the information and content.

Organizing Heirarchy Based on Sales Funnel
It was always drilled into my brain, "Always have a reason why." When tackling this project I took the main goal of scheduling clients for a chat and thought about how I can structure this website to capture these leads. There will be clients who will convert instantly. So we want a way to make Scheduling an Appointment seem effortless and the very first call to action we see. We also have clients that are not wise to what services are offered by a probate lawyer so we want a section to Shocase Services Provided by Dante. There might be users who won't be convinced until they see proof that Dante is indeed Knowledgeable. And Lastly we want Social Proof that sings Dante's praises for those who are still researching in the sales funnel.

Home
The concept with this approach is we present data to the user in the oder that we find will promise the most conversions. Conversions in this case refers to how many scheduled appointments Dante gets. Clicking on the top right Call to Action of the button in the hero image will take them to a Calendly scheduling flow where the user will choose a time and date of when to chat with Dante.

Articles
Dante has a ton of great content in an effort to educate the user as well as prove he is knowlegeable in his field. He writes weekly blogs on subjects pertaining to his services and I believe this could be a great sales proposition that can help users convert to clients.

Articles
Dante offers a variety of different servies but much of the two packages he offers have overlap. I found that this approach was a clear way to know exactly what you get when you ask for a certain package. Also clicking on the services provided will take you to the corresponding blog article Dante wrote.

Testimonials
Much like a lot of goods and services today we rely on peer review before making a purchase. This section will be real data taken from Google Reviews and displayed here. Past happy customers will help convert new ones.

Footer
I wanted the footer to be utilitatian as well as a last ditch effort to get something from the user. This case an email so that Dante can email his content and keep his name fresh in their minds. I also wanted this to be a place where popular articles can be accessed.

Variants
Versioning is important. I built a visual framework piece-mealing styling components derived from the stylyguide so that Dante can see how we can modularly build this website.
