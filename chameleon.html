The Challenge

This project was during my time at Google on the Shopping Ads Teams. Our ads live on different surfaces including: Image Search, Youtube and Discovery app but when we surface our ads on Search there are approvals and alignment that needs to take place because our ads sit at the top of the page and push down everything else below the page. For this project we explored what happens when a query doesn't return text ads (Search Team's ads) and how we can utilize the extra space when they don't trigger.

Production
Before in production when you search "march birthstone jewelery" you find our ads sitting at the top and Text Ads sitting right below them. There is an agreement between both teams that states that the Shopping Ads would not exceed a certain height as it pushes the Text Ads lower on the page. The idea being the items lower on the page take scrolling to get to so it would drive down Click Through Rate. Below we have a diagram showing the two ads formats living on the same surface.

Concept
There will be times when a search query will not return text ads so there will be more vertical space to work with for Shopping Ads. How can we utilize this space to better serve the user?

The Execution
The idea behind this concept is that in the event a Text Ad will not trigger on the page an accordion UI will trigger underneath the Shopping Ads unit. By clicking on the accordion a new slate of offers would open based on the category you choose. These categories are adjacent requeries based on machine learning and geared towards broadening the user's original search query.

Progressive Disclosure
The idea of Progressive Disclosure means that since the user has clicked on the accordion in this case, that should signal to Google that the user is intentionally broadening their search query therefore it is okay to show more data. And by showing more data we mean showing more offer cards and additional accordions.

This project has since gone into experiment with a big slice of shoppable search queries and has returned with positive impressions.
